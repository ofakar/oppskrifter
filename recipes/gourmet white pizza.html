Gourmet White Pizza with Garlic-Parmesan Sauce, Mushrooms & Spinach

Makes: 2 large pizzas (~35 cm each) or 3-4 medium pizzas (~28 cm each)

Ingredients

Base Ingredients:

  • Pizza dough - 1 kg (divided as needed)
  • Olive oil - 2 tbsp (to brush the crust)
  • White sauce - Full batch (about 500 ml)

Toppings:

  • Mushrooms - 200 g, thinly sliced (cremini, portobello, or shiitake)
  • Fresh spinach - 100 g, roughly chopped
  • Mozzarella - 200 g, torn (fresh) or shredded
  • Parmesan - 60 g, grated
  • Shallots - 2 small, finely sliced
  • Prosciutto - 6-8 slices (optional)
  • Black pepper - Freshly ground, to taste
  • Red pepper flakes - A pinch (optional)
  • Truffle oil - A drizzle (optional)

Instructions

1. Preheat & Prepare:

Preheat the oven to 250°C (or as hot as it goes). If using a pizza stone, heat it for at least 30 minutes.

2. Cook the Mushrooms & Spinach:

  • Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a pan.
  • Sauté mushrooms and shallots with a pinch of salt until golden (~5-6 minutes).
  • Add spinach for the last 30 seconds, just to wilt it. Set aside.

3. Roll Out the Dough:

  • Divide the 1 kg dough based on your pizza sizes (2 large or 3-4 medium).
  • Roll/stretch each portion to 28-35 cm, depending on your preference.

4. Assemble the Pizzas:

  • Spread white sauce evenly over each pizza (~125-150 ml per pizza).
  • Distribute mozzarella over the sauce.
  • Evenly scatter sautéed mushrooms, shallots, and spinach.
  • Lay prosciutto in thin strips (if using).
  • Sprinkle with Parmesan, black pepper, and red pepper flakes.

5. Bake:

  • Transfer pizzas to a preheated stone or baking sheet.
  • Bake for 7-10 minutes until the crust is golden and crisp.
  • Stone-baked pizzas may take only 5-7 minutes.

6. Finish & Serve:

  • Drizzle with truffle oil (optional, but amazing with mushrooms).
  • Slice and serve immediately!

Tips for the Best White Pizza:

  • To prevent sogginess, don't overload the pizza with sauce or toppings.
  • If you like a crispy crust, pre-bake the dough for 2-3 minutes before adding toppings.
  • Experiment with other cheeses like Fontina or Gorgonzola for more flavor depth.

Wine Pairing:

  • White Wine: A dry Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio.
  • Red Wine: A light Pinot Noir or Chianti.
